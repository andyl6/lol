Here are some places in NYC that I enjoyed.

Spot Address
Meow Parlour: Cat cafe in Manhattan. Nice place to see cats and relax. 46 Hester St, New York, NY 10002
Softbite: Cute date place. Presentable dishes like Souffle and salad. Also opens late for drinkers. 42-60 Crescent St, Queens, NY 11101
Little Island: Nice place for sunset view, good food nearby. Pier 55 in Hudson River Park at, W 13th St, New York, NY 10014
New Wonjo: Korean restaurant. Appetizers are good and they give you watermelon if you tip well. Good menu too 23 W 32nd St, New York, NY 10001
Ajisen Ramen: Multiple locations. I go to the one listed. Super reliable & mad cheap 14 Mott St, New York, NY 10013
Long Island Railroad (LIRR): Not really a place, but it's good to go to places outside of NYC for a good getaway, i.e Round 1 @ Hicksville. Penn Station/Atlantic Ave

My personal thoughts

NYC is a pretty nice place to go. There's a lot of places to eat, most attractions in my opinion are for tourists. The Edge is an example, but that place is infested with red lantern flies so it's really ugly. I haven't really gone past Times Square but I have been to some nice food spots and whatnot. It's somewhat dangerous at night because that is when most weird people and rats come out (ktown), but it is also quite nice at night. Karaoke, eating out with friends, and Times Square are all nice places in the night. But there are also places like museums and parks if you are that type of person. I personally enjoy drinking so I mostly know places to go out to eat and whatnot. The only real issues are transit problems and that most of the interesting stuff is in Manhattan. But, NY is also near some nice places like American Dream in NJ and Round 1 Arcade a few cities to the right. It's near some colleges too. Other than that, I think NYC is a nice place to visit and has a good amount of things to do.
